Section 03 — Chain of Command

Alliance Ranks

R1 to R5. Most of it is prestige, not power — but the difference between an R1 and an R4 is the difference between a passenger and a driver. Here's what each rank actually does, and where the real authority lives.

R5
The Leader

Full control. Builds the HQ, sets the banner, names and disbands, runs diplomacy. There's exactly one. Heads up: if an R5 goes dark for 3 days, the R4s can launch a takeover — that's how dead alliances get inherited on older states.

R4
Senior Officer — The Engine

The real workhorses. Kick, promote, start alliance tech, place banners, post the bulletin, set event times, and lead rallies. In most alliances an R4 carries the same load as the R5. This is where leadership actually happens.

R3
Officer

Can promote and demote R1s and R2s. Often used to recognise your most active, trusted members — the ones allowed to ping @All. Limited slots, so it carries some weight.

R2
Trusted Member

Functionally identical to R3 in permissions. A marker that you've proven yourself active and reliable. No tangible perks over R1 beyond standing.

R1
Recruit

Entry rank. Chat, contribute to tech, tap helps, view profiles, and leave if you want. Be warned — if the alliance has to cut deadweight, R1s are first on the chopping block. Stay active and you won't be one for long.
